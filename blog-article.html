10 Hidden Gems in Paris Only Locals Know

By Marie Laurent · April 5, 2026 · 6 min read · Paris
Share: Twitter · Facebook · Pinterest · Copy Link

Paris is a city that rewards curiosity. Beyond the Eiffel Tower and Louvre, a world of hidden courtyards, secret passages, and local haunts awaits those willing to venture off the beaten path. As private tour guides who've spent decades exploring every arrondissement, here are our favourite hidden gems that most tourists never discover.

1. Passage des Panoramas

Built in 1799, this is Paris's oldest covered passage and one of its best-kept secrets. Tucked between Boulevard Montmartre and Rue Saint-Marc, it houses vintage stamp dealers, quirky bistros, and a beautifully preserved 19th-century atmosphere. The ironwork galleries and mosaic floors are a window into a Paris that has otherwise largely disappeared.

Passage des Panoramas Interior

2. Musee de la Chasse et de la Nature

Housed in a magnificent Marais mansion, this quirky museum combines fine art with natural history in unexpected ways. It's rarely crowded, beautifully curated, and completely unlike any museum you've visited before. Room after room reveals taxidermied animals in conversation with Old Master paintings, contemporary artworks tucked inside antique armoires, and an atmosphere of elegant strangeness.

3. Rue Cremieux

This Instagram-worthy street near Gare de Lyon features colourful pastel houses that look like they belong in Notting Hill. It's a two-minute detour that yields some of the best photos of your trip. Early morning is the ideal time to visit — before the day-trippers arrive and when the light falls softly between the facades.

Rue Cremieux Colourful Houses

4. Parc des Buttes-Chaumont

This dramatic 19th-century park in the 19th arrondissement features cliffs, waterfalls, a lake, and a Greek-style temple perched on an island. It's where Parisians go to escape the crowds — families spread out on the grass on weekends, runners circle the lake at dawn, and in summer the whole park hums with the kind of unhurried pleasure that defines the Parisian art of living.

5. Le Marais Jewish Quarter

The Pletzl, as locals call it, is centred around Rue des Rosiers. Sample the best falafel in Paris, browse independent bookshops, and discover centuries of Jewish heritage in this vibrant neighbourhood. The quarter survived the Nazi occupation and today stands as both a living community and a monument to resilience.

6. Square du Vert-Galant

Tucked at the very tip of the Ile de la Cite, this slender triangular park descends to the level of the Seine like the prow of a ship. It's one of the most romantic spots in the city — willows trailing into the water, riverboats gliding past, the bridges of Paris framed on either side. Most visitors cross the Pont Neuf above without ever noticing the steps that lead down to this hidden garden.

7. Catacombs — Beyond the Official Tour

While the official catacombs see a million visitors a year, the network of tunnels beneath Paris stretches for nearly 300 kilometres. Authorised tours with specialist guides explore sections rarely seen by the public — including rooms adorned with 18th-century stonecutters' art and the eerie silence of tunnels untouched for generations.
